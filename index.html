The Blood Donation Process

1

Registration

You'll complete donor registration which includes providing your ID and basic information.

2

Health History

You'll answer some questions about your health history and places you've traveled.

3

Mini-Physical

We'll check your temperature, pulse, blood pressure and hemoglobin level.

4

Donation

The actual blood donation takes about 8-10 minutes for a whole blood donation.

5

Refreshment

After donating, you'll enjoy snacks and drinks while you rest for 10-15 minutes.

Donor Eligibility Requirements

Basic Requirements

  • Be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Be in good general health

Wait Periods

  • 56 days between whole blood donations
  • 7 days between platelet donations
  • 112 days between double red cell donations

Temporary Deferrals

  • Cold, flu or fever - until symptom-free
  • Pregnancy - 6 weeks after delivery
  • Travel to malaria-risk areas - 3 month deferral

Frequently Asked Questions

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made every 7 days up to 24 times per year. Double red cell donations can be made every 112 days (16 weeks).

Does donating blood hurt?

You may feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but the donation process itself is generally painless. Most donors compare it to a slight pinch that lasts just a second.

How long does a blood donation take?

The actual blood donation typically takes about 8-10 minutes for a whole blood donation. The entire process from registration to refreshments takes about 45 minutes to an hour.

Is donating blood safe?

Yes, donating blood is very safe. All equipment is sterile, used only once, and then discarded. There's no risk of contracting any infectious disease from donating blood.

How much blood is taken?

For a whole blood donation, approximately one pint (about 500ml) is collected. This represents less than 10% of the average adult's total blood volume, which your body replaces within 24-48 hours.
